Alan Turing(1912–1954)

Alan Turing is known as the "father of modern computer science and AI" , he is also known for famously leading the cracking of the German Enigma code at Bletchley Park.

Did you know?-If you type Bletchley Park in Google, you'll get a special surprise at the top of the page.

How did he crack the Enigma Machine?

The Enigma machine was a cipher device which was used to to encrypt millitary communications. It had over 159 quintillion possible settings.

Turing and his Allied codebreaking team found out a flaw in the machine , which meant that they didn't have consider every single possible key.

He also improved a Polish design to create a machine that used logical, apid testing to find the settings of the Enigma's rotors and plugboards called the Bombe Machine.

Using the information they found out in the once encrypted code, they passed on the information to the Navy and Air force to help avoid U-boats. But to keep the Germans from realizing they had cracked the Enigma, they sometimes allowed attackes.
