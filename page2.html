Historic Figures in Computing
Tim Berners-Lee(born 8 June 1955)

Tim Berners-Lee is well known for creating the World Wide Web(WWW), HTML, the URL system, and HTTP.

He is an English computer scientist and a professorial research fellow at the University of Oxford.

How he created the World Wide Web

From June to December 1980, Berners-Lee worked as and independent contractor at CERN

There, he proposed a project based on the concept of hypertext so that researchers could update and share information among themselves. And to demonstrate that idea, he built a prototype system called ENQUIRE.

In 1989, CERN was the largest internet Node in Europe. That's when Berners-Lee saw an oppurtunity to join hypertext with the internet.

Did you know? - That he was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II in 2004 for his groundbreaking work!

With Robert Cailliau as partner (he had separately proposed a project about hypertext), they both worked together to get the web off the ground. They used similar ideas underlying the ENQUIRE system to create the WWW.
